## Overview

This research develops a 3D Discrete Element Method (DEM) framework written in Fortran F90 to simulate how varying degrees of soil saturation affect the packing structure and mechanical behavior of clay particles. The framework incorporates full DLVO physicochemical force models to capture the complex interactions between clay platelets.

## Key Contributions

- **Fortran F90 Implementation**: High-performance DEM solver optimized for particle-scale simulations
- **Saturation-Dependent Forces**: Full DLVO force implementation including van der Waals attraction, electrical double layer repulsion, and capillary bridge forces for partial saturation
- **Clay Platelet Geometry**: Models realistic platelet shapes with aspect ratios representative of kaolinite minerals
- **Packing Analysis**: Studies how saturation state affects void ratio, fabric tensor, and particle arrangement

## Current Results

The framework correctly reproduces the expected force ordering across saturation states: partial saturation generates the strongest net attractive forces, followed by dry conditions, with fully saturated conditions producing the weakest attraction — directly influencing particle packing density and fabric orientation.
