## Background

The September 2018 Mw 7.5 Palu earthquake in Sulawesi, Indonesia triggered extraordinary flowslides that traveled several kilometers over near-flat terrain, destroying entire neighborhoods.

## Research Objective

This work aims to numerically reproduce the observed runout behavior using particle-based methods (SPH and MPM), with particular attention to the role of pore water pressure generation and dissipation during the flow process.

## Approach

- Characterize the pre-failure soil conditions using available geotechnical site investigation data
- Implement strain-softening constitutive models appropriate for the Palu soils
- Compare SPH and MPM predictions of runout distance, velocity, and deposit morphology
- Validate against satellite imagery and field measurements
